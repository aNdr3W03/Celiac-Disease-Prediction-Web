Typical Celiac or "Classic Celiac Disease" refers to a form of celiac disease in which patients experience typical symptoms associated with problems with digestion and nutrient absorption. These symptoms appear in response to consuming gluten, a protein found in grains, wheat, barley, spelt, kamut, and rye. The following are some symptoms that may occur to you as a "Typical Celiac" patient,

  1. Digestive Symptoms
    Patients with this typical celiac type often experience digestive problems such as diarrhea, nausea, constipation, bloating and abdominal pain. These symptoms occur because gluten damages the lining of the small intestine, which reduces the body's ability to absorb nutrients properly.
  2. Nutritional Deficiencies
    Because damage to the small intestine, especially the villi which are responsible for absorbing nutrients, actually inhibits the absorption of nutrients, patient with this type can experience nutritional deficiencies, including deficiencies of iron, calcium, vitamin D, and vitamin B12.
  3. Weight Loss
    Due to damage in the small intestine, the body cannot absorb nutrients efficiently, patient with Typical Celiac disease tend to experience unwanted weight loss.
  4. Fatigue
    Patient with the Typical Celiac disease often feel very tired and chronically fatigued, which is caused by nutritional deficiencies and poor absorption as a result of damage to the villi in the small intestine.
  5. External Symptoms
    Some people with this type may also experience skin symptoms, such as a rash (dermatitis herpetiformis), which is a typical sign of celiac disease. This rash can be very itchy and affect certain areas of the skin.

Diagnosis of Typical Celiac disease usually involves a series of blood tests that you may have done to detect celiac disease-specific antibodies, such as anti-tTG IgA (anti-tissue Transglutaminase Immunoglobulin A) and anti-EMA IgA (anti-Endomysial Antibodies Immunoglobulin A). If the blood test results are suspicious, a small intestine biopsy is usually performed to confirm the diagnosis.

It is important to note that management of classic type celiac disease involves a lifelong gluten-free diet. By avoiding gluten completely, symptoms can be managed and damage to the small intestine can be stopped. Foods containing grains, wheat, barley, spelt, kamut, and rye should be avoided completely, and you often need the help of a nutritionist to ensure they are getting adequate nutrition from a gluten-free diet.
